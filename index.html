Projects

  • HPOLabeler: improving prediction of human protein-phenotype associations by learning to rank
    • We proposed HPOLabeler, which integrates diverse data sources and multiple basic models in the framework of "Stacking method" in ensemble learning and improves the performance by Learning to Rank, to predict the HPO (Human Phenotype Ontology) annotations of human proteins.
    • AUPR is improved by near 20% in cross-validation, and our model is the only one with better performance than Naive method.
    • The paper has been published on Bioinformatics, and accepted as oral talk in the Function COSI at ISMB 2020.
    • Online platform: http://issubmission.sjtu.edu.cn/hpolabeler/
  • Intelligent collection technology of breeding data of Kuruma prawn and Pseudosciaena crocea
    • We collected a large number of images of Kuruma prawn for the research on the image detection technology of prawn heads.
    • Inspired by Adaboost-based face detection algorithms, we trained a cascade classifier by Haar features of prawn.
    • The project was finished by OpenCV C++.
  • Multi-drone cluster performance
    • Multi-UAV cluster performance was based on Crazyflie four-axis UAV.
    • Multiple drones coordinate with each other to complete performance according to scheduling. Drones can form spirals, fountains and other shapes in the air, with flashing lights.
    • Trajectory and formation are controlled by our Python-coded client program.
